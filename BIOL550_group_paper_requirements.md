# BIOL550 Group Paper Requirements and Draft Submission Guide

This document summarizes the official requirements and expectations for the BIOL550 group paper, as communicated by Dr. Osier and the course syllabus. It is intended as a persistent reference for all group members to ensure compliance and reproducibility.

---

## Draft Submission Schedule (Weeks 13–15)

- **Week 13:** Outline of the paper
- **Week 14:** Draft of half of the text
- **Week 15:** Draft of the rest of the paper

**Submission:**
- Submit to the appropriate myCourses DropBox **before Thursday workshop**
- Each draft is worth 1% of the final grade (3% total)
- **No late drafts accepted for a grade**
- Only one group member needs to submit per draft

**Formatting for Drafts:**
- Use **MS Word (2016 or later), LibreOffice, or OpenOffice** (no Google Docs)
- **Color your individual text** in a unique color (per group member)
- **Include a comment at the start** with your full name and color for identification

---

## Paper Formatting Requirements

- **Length:** 30–50 pages
- **Primary sources:** Minimum 15
- **Figures/tables:** No more than half the paper; remainder must be prose
- **Paper size:** 8.5" x 11"
- **Spacing:** Double-spaced
- **Font:** 10 or 12 pt Calibri or Times New Roman
- **Margins:** Exactly 1"
- **Citations:** Do not count toward page requirements
- **Direct quotes:** No more than one page total
- **Sections:** Introduction, Materials and Methods, Results, Discussion, References
- **No abstracts**
- **Proper spelling, grammar, and style**

---

## Draft Formatting Notes

- One clear header per section
- Use consistent bullet styles
- Use numbered lists only when order matters
- Leave white space for quick scanning and edits

---

## Scientific and Reproducibility Expectations

- **Cite all tools used** (start gathering citations early)
- **Include a table of samples** in Materials and Methods with all known details
- **Give sample commands for every tool used** (list parameters changed from default)
- **For Results figures/tables:**
  - In prose, describe what is interesting (outliers, patterns, unexpected findings)
  - Give hard numbers where possible
  - Report factual findings in Results; interpretations in Discussion

---

## Draft Feedback Process
- All tools with version number
- Feedback will be given as general suggestions to the group
- Inline feedback requires editable formats (not PDF)
- Only one group member needs to submit each draft
